Platforms.

These are where AI meets small business operations. The platform you choose shapes everything — cost, flexibility, who can maintain it, and how fast you can build.

The three main players

When to use each

Use Zapier when...
The client needs to maintain it themselves, the workflow is simple (trigger → 1–2 steps → output), and you need one of Zapier's 6,000+ pre-built integrations with no custom code.
Use Make when...
The workflow has complex branching, data needs transforming mid-flow, or you need to handle errors gracefully. The visual builder is excellent for showing clients what's happening.
Use n8n when...
Volume is high (per-task pricing would be punishing), the team is technical, you want full control over AI prompt logic, or the client wants to self-host for data privacy reasons.

Also worth knowing

LangChain

A Python framework for building RAG pipelines and agent chains in code. More flexible than the no-code tools but requires a developer. Best for teams that are already Python-native and need complex multi-agent orchestration. Often overkill — evaluate n8n first.

LlamaIndex

Similar to LangChain but specifically focused on data ingestion and RAG. Better than LangChain for "connect AI to a big pile of documents" use cases. Same caveat: code-first, developer required.

GoHighLevel

An all-in-one marketing platform that includes CRM, email, SMS, and missed call text-back. Popular with marketing agencies selling AI automation to local businesses. Not as flexible as n8n but much easier for non-technical clients.
