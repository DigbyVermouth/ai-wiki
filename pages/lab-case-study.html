The Problem: The $50,000 hole in every contractor's business

Picture a plumber. He's under a sink at 2pm, hands full of pipe fittings, and his phone rings. He can't answer. The caller gets voicemail. 85% of callers who reach voicemail don't leave a message. They hang up and call the next plumber on Google.

Now multiply that:

27% of incoming calls go unanswered at the average home service business
$1,200 average value of a missed plumbing call (emergency jobs command 1.5–2× rates)
$50K+ estimated annual revenue lost by average plumbing business to missed calls

The brutal math: a plumber spending $500/month on Google Ads to generate calls — and missing 27% of them — is throwing away $135/month in marketing spend on top of the lost jobs. The problem sells itself.

And it's not just plumbers. HVAC, electricians, landscapers, roofers, salon owners, lawyers, dentists — any local business where the owner is doing the work and can't always answer the phone has this exact problem.

The Solution: Missed Call Text-Back + AI Qualification

The core product is simple enough to explain in one sentence: when a call is missed, the system automatically texts the caller back within 30–60 seconds and starts a qualifying conversation via SMS.

Here's what actually happens step by step:

1
Call is missed

The business owner's phone rings while he's on a job. He can't answer. Normally, this is a dead lead. With the system, it triggers an automation.

2
Instant text-back (30–60 seconds)

The caller's number receives an automatic SMS: "Hi! This is Thompson Plumbing — sorry we missed you. We're on a job right now. What can we help you with?" Branded, human-sounding, immediate. The caller is still on their phone.

3
AI qualification via SMS

The caller responds. The AI (trained on the business's services, service area, and pricing) asks qualifying questions: "Is this an emergency or can it wait? What's the issue? Where are you located?" It handles the conversation naturally, one message at a time.

4
Lead qualification & routing

The AI determines: is this a qualified lead in the service area? Is it an emergency that needs immediate routing? It summarizes the conversation and either books an appointment, forwards details to the owner's phone, or pushes into the CRM automatically.

5
Owner reviews qualified lead

The owner finishes the job, checks his phone, and sees: "New lead: Sarah M., burst pipe, 123 Oak St (your service area), needs emergency response, contact info captured." He calls back a warm, pre-qualified lead instead of a cold unknown caller.

The Architecture (what's actually running)

What's under the hood
Component What it does Typical tech
Call detection Detects missed call, triggers workflow Twilio, VoIP forwarding
SMS dispatch Sends initial text-back within 60s Twilio SMS, 10DLC compliant
AI conversation Qualifies lead via back-and-forth SMS Claude or GPT-4o via API, system prompt with business context
CRM integration Pushes qualified lead into job management Jobber, HousecallPro, ServiceTitan (MCP or API)
Workflow orchestration Connects all the pieces n8n, Make, or custom Node.js
The AI part is actually small Notice that the LLM is just one step in a larger workflow. The heavy lifting is the call detection, SMS delivery (10DLC compliance is a real operational challenge), and CRM integration. This is true of most real AI products — the AI is the brain, but the plumbing is the work.

The Business Model: Why this works to sell

This product has near-perfect product-market fit for a simple reason: the ROI is obvious and immediate. You don't have to convince a plumber that missed calls cost him money — he already knows and it already hurts. You're just solving a problem he can already feel.

The sales pitch writes itself:

"You're missing 27% of your calls. That's probably 5–10 jobs a month at $500–1,200 each. This system texts them back in 30 seconds, qualifies them while you're working, and drops the lead straight into your job board. It costs $99 a month. If it captures one extra job, it paid for itself 5 times over."

The math at scale: if you sign up 100 contractors at $99/month, that's $9,900/month recurring revenue. At $297/month (higher tier), 100 contractors = $29,700 MRR. The underlying API costs (Twilio + LLM per conversation) are typically $2–8 per month per customer at average volume. Margins are very strong.
