Study Plan · Weekend

This
Weekend.

You have roughly 48 hours. Here's the highest-ROI sequence — designed around how you said you study: broad overview, then deep dives during mental breaks.

Your study style, honored You said you take mental breaks and go broad, then return to deep study. This plan is built exactly for that: the overview items are short and give you the map, the deep dives are there when you want to go further.

Saturday — build the mental model

1
Read: The Stack (10 min)

Get the 5-layer mental model. Everything else hangs off this. Don't skip it even though it's short.

2
Read: Tier 1 Terms (20 min)

12 terms. Read, cover, say it in your own words. Repeat for any that don't stick.

3
Deep dive break: LLMs page (15 min)

The "what LLMs are bad at" section is the most important. Those are Monday's gotcha opportunities.

4
Read: Lab Case Study (25 min)

The contractor AI product. Read it like a business case — understand the problem, the ROI math, and why the architecture is what it is.

5
Deep dive break: RAG page (15 min)

After the case study, RAG will click immediately — you've just seen it in action.

6
Read: Lab Build & Pitch (20 min)

The professional services angle. This is your Monday contribution if the conversation goes to new product ideas.

Sunday — sharpen the edges

7
Read: The Gotcha Guide (20 min)

Study each trap and its redirect. Say the redirect out loud — it needs to feel natural, not rehearsed.

8
Deep dive breaks: Agents, MCP, Prompting

Pick whichever feels weakest. Each page stands alone — read in any order.

9
Review: Tier 2 Terms (10 min)

Scan them. You don't need to memorize — you need recognition. If you hear them Monday, you should have a sense of what they mean.

10
Check: Anthropic's official resources

Download the AI Fluency cheat sheet PDF and bookmark anthropic.com/learn. These are free and excellent cross-references.

The real goal by Monday You don't need to know everything. You need to: (1) have the mental model so nothing sounds alien, (2) know the Tier 1 terms cold, (3) understand one real product end-to-end, and (4) have three or four gotcha redirects ready. That's fluency.
