The Real-World
Lab.

Reading about AI and building with AI are completely different experiences. This lab walks you through a real product — from problem to architecture to pitch — so Monday's meeting feels like review, not discovery.

What the lab covers

📋 Proven Case Study
A deeply researched walkthrough of one of the most successfully marketed AI products for small businesses right now: AI missed call text-back for contractors. Real numbers, real architecture, real competitors.
🔧 Build & Pitch
Taking the proven product and identifying a better-fit, underserved market — professional services (lawyers, accountants). Full architecture, system prompt example, cost estimate, and a 5-slide pitch.
🏗️ Architecture Walkthrough
The actual technical stack: Twilio + Claude API + n8n + CRM integration. Every component explained, every cost estimated. You'll understand what's actually running under the hood.

Why this approach

The fastest way to internalize AI terminology is to see it in context. When you've walked through a real system that uses RAG, function calling, system prompts, and n8n workflows — those terms stop being abstract and start being things you've seen working.

By the end of the lab, you'll be able to say in Monday's meeting: "We'd need RAG for the knowledge base, a system prompt that handles conflict checking, and probably n8n to orchestrate the Twilio and CRM integrations." That sentence — spoken naturally — is what fluency sounds like.

Lab sequence Do the Case Study first (see how a proven product works), then Build & Pitch (apply the thinking to a new angle), then Architecture (go as deep as you want on the technical layer).
