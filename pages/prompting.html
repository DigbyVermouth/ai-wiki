Prompting &
System Prompts.

The most immediately useful skill in AI. A great prompt is the difference between a confused intern and a brilliant consultant — using the exact same model.

The anatomy of a prompt

Every interaction with an LLM has up to three parts: the system prompt (persistent background instructions, set by the developer, invisible to the user), the conversation history (the back-and-forth so far), and the user message (what the user just typed). The LLM sees all three simultaneously and generates its response from the combined context.

System prompts: where products are built

The system prompt is where a raw LLM becomes a specific product. It defines the persona, the rules, the knowledge, and the output format. For small business AI, the system prompt is your most important engineering artifact — not the model choice, not the infrastructure.

EXAMPLE SYSTEM PROMPT — AI INTAKE FOR LAW FIRM
You are the after-hours intake assistant for Smith & Associates Law in Salt Lake City. You handle personal injury, family law, and estate planning matters in Utah.

Your goal: gather intake information warmly and professionally. Never provide legal advice. Ask one question at a time. Gather: type of matter, brief description, urgency level, full name, best contact time.

If the caller mentions any of these existing client names [list], flag as potential conflict and do not continue intake — say a team member will call them directly.

End every conversation by confirming: "We'll have someone reach out within one business day. Is there anything else you need right now?"

Tone: warm, professional, reassuring. Sentences under 30 words. Never use legal jargon.

Notice what this does: it defines role, scope, rules, process, conflict handling, closing behavior, and tone — all before the user types a single word. That's a product, not a chat session.

The Anthropic "4 Ds" framework (from their official cheat sheet)

Anthropic released a free AI Fluency cheat sheet built around four competencies. These map directly to how you write great prompts:

Delegation — what should AI do vs. humans?

Before prompting, decide: is this task appropriate for AI? What's the failure mode if it gets it wrong? Who reviews the output? Prompts for high-stakes outputs need explicit human review steps built in.

Description — tell it exactly what you want

Specify the output format, the audience, the length, the tone. "Write a follow-up email" is weak. "Write a 3-sentence follow-up email to a plumbing customer who requested an estimate last week, warm and professional, ending with a specific call to action" is strong.

Discernment — evaluate what it produces

Don't accept first outputs for anything important. Read critically. Ask: is this accurate? Is it appropriate? Did it follow all the instructions? The best AI users iterate — they treat the first output as a draft, not a deliverable.

Diligence — use it responsibly

Be transparent about AI involvement when it matters. Verify factual claims. Take responsibility for AI-assisted work you put your name on. Don't let AI output go out the door unchecked.
