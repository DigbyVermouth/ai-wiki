Study Plan · This Week

After
Monday.

Monday will reveal what direction matters most. This week's plan branches based on what you learn. Here's how to think about each path.

The branching principle Don't try to go deep on everything — you'll go shallow on all of it. Monday will surface 1–2 areas that are most relevant to your role. Go deep on those, stay at overview level on the rest.

If the meeting goes toward building products / apps

Week focus: APIs, prompt engineering, cost modeling

Take Anthropic's free API course at anthropic.com/learn. Learn to make a direct API call with your own system prompt. Understand token pricing and how to estimate costs for a real product. Read: "Building with Claude" in Anthropic's docs.

Key skills: writing effective system prompts, handling conversation history in API calls, structured output (JSON mode), error handling.

If the meeting goes toward automation & workflows

Week focus: n8n hands-on, Twilio basics, CRM integrations

Set up a free n8n cloud account. Build one workflow: "when I fill out a Typeform, Claude summarizes it and emails it to me." This single build will teach you webhooks, API calls, and data passing — the three core skills of automation.

Then look up Twilio's free trial and make one SMS send work. Once you've sent a text programmatically, the whole contractor product architecture will click.

If the meeting goes toward AI strategy

Week focus: case studies, ROI frameworks, evaluations

Read McKinsey's State of AI report (free). Study 3–5 real small business AI case studies — search "AI for [industry] case study 2025." Learn how to build a simple ROI calculator for an AI product: time saved × hourly rate + revenue recovered − tool costs = monthly value.

Also worth learning: how "evals" work — how teams systematically test AI outputs for quality before shipping. This is the most mature teams' secret weapon.

Regardless of direction — do these this week

1
Take Anthropic's AI Fluency course

Free, online, takes 2–3 hours, earns a certificate. anthropic.com/learn — "AI Fluency: Framework and Foundations." Excellent complement to this guide.

2
Explore Anthropic's prompt engineering tutorial

github.com/anthropics/prompt-eng-interactive-tutorial — interactive, runs in Google Sheets. Goes from basic structure to advanced techniques. Hands-on is the fastest way to internalize prompting.

3
Build one small thing

Doesn't matter what. A workflow that solves a real problem you have. The act of building forces you to encounter real problems (authentication, error handling, formatting) that make every concept concrete.

4
Update this wiki

After Monday, you'll know what terms came up that aren't here. Add them. The best study resource is one you've personalized.
